Welcome to the Free State

The Free State, South Africa's agricultural heartland, offers wide open spaces, golden fields, and warm hospitality. From the historic Battlefields to the Maluti Mountains, from the charming small towns to the vibrant city of Bloemfontein, the Free State promises authentic South African experiences. Our comprehensive guide helps you plan every aspect of your trip.

Top Destinations

Bloemfontein

Capital City

The judicial capital of South Africa, known as the "City of Roses," with beautiful parks, museums, and historical sites.

Entrance Fees: R50-R150
Accommodation: R600-R2000/night
Transport: Flights, buses, car hire

Top Activities:

  • Visit Naval Hill and Franklin Game Reserve
  • Explore Anglo-Boer War Museum
  • Stroll through Kings Park Rose Garden
  • Tour the Oliewenhuis Art Museum
  • Shop at Loch Logan Waterfront

Golden Gate Highlands

Eastern Free State

Spectacular sandstone formations that glow golden in the sunset, offering hiking trails and breathtaking scenery.

Park Fees: R80 per adult
Accommodation: R800-R2500/night
Transport: Car recommended

Top Activities:

  • Hiking various scenic trails
  • Photography of golden rock formations
  • Horse riding through the park
  • Bird watching (over 140 species)
  • Visit Basotho Cultural Village

Clarens

Eastern Free State

Charming artists' village nestled in the foothills of the Maluti Mountains, known for galleries and outdoor activities.

Transport: Car or shuttle from Bloem

Vredefort Dome

Northern Free State

World's oldest and largest meteorite impact site, now a UNESCO World Heritage Site with unique geological formations.

Entrance Fees: R60 per adult
Accommodation: R500-R1500/night
Transport: Car from Johannesburg

Top Activities:

  • Guided geological tours
  • Hiking along impact ridges
  • River rafting on Vaal River
  • Visit museum and info center
  • Photography of unique landscapes
Book Now

Travel Tips & Advice

Best Time to Visit

Spring (September-November) for wildflowers and mild weather. Autumn (March-May) for golden landscapes. Winter brings crisp, clear days perfect for hiking.

Getting Around

Renting a car is ideal for exploring the Free State's wide open spaces. Major towns are well connected by road. Domestic flights serve Bloemfontein.

Local Cuisine

Try traditional Free State dishes like potjiekos, boerewors, and melktert. The region is known for its excellent lamb and artisanal cheeses.

Outdoor Activities

Hiking, horse riding, fishing, and birdwatching are popular. The Eastern Highlands offer spectacular trails and adventure activities.

Historical Sites

Explore Anglo-Boer War battlefields and museums. The Free State played a crucial role in South Africa's history.

Shopping

Look for handmade crafts, wool products, and art in towns like Clarens. Bloemfontein offers excellent shopping malls.
